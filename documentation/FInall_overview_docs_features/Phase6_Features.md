## 2Ô∏è‚É£ SELF-PACED LEARNING (NEW & SEPARATE)

> Self-paced learning is **non-mandatory**, **non-linear**, and **always accessible**.

### Definition

**Self-Paced Learning** consists of:

* Additional content
* Notes
* Reference material
* Explanatory videos
* Practice resources

Uploaded by **Faculty**, intended for **independent study**.

---

### 2.1 Student Access to Self-Paced Content

Students can:

* Access a dedicated **‚ÄúSelf-Paced Learning‚Äù section**
* View content grouped by:

  * Subject
  * Course
  * Faculty
* Open content anytime (no locking)
* Revisit content even after course completion

Restrictions:

* Self-paced content:

  * Does **not** affect course completion
  * Does **not** unlock steps
  * Is **not graded**
  * Has **no prerequisites**

---

### 2.2 Progress Handling (Self-Paced)

* No mandatory completion
* Optional ‚ÄúViewed‚Äù status may be tracked
* No percentage contribution to course progress
* No blocking or enforcement

---

### 2.3 Visibility Rules

* Students see **only self-paced content**:

  * Uploaded by faculty of their college
  * Mapped to their subjects/courses
* Content remains visible:

  * Even if course is completed
  * Until faculty/admin archives it

## 1Ô∏è‚É£ SELF-PACED CONTENT DEFINITION

Self-paced content includes:

* Non-graded

## 2Ô∏è‚É£ FACULTY CAPABILITIES

* Define metadata:

* Archive self-paced content

Restrictions:

  * Their subjects

* Package violations
* Progress manipulation

## 4Ô∏è‚É£ STORAGE & ACCESS RULES

* Archiving removes student access but preserves logs
